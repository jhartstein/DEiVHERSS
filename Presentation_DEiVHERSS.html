Executive Summary

Recommendation for blended reporting

  • prepare printable reports, easy-access html summaries, presentations
  • include live-interaction with dashboards
  • combine reporting and computation in a joint environment
  • use templates with corporate design elements
  • do this in an Open Science way with R, RMarkdown and R/Shiny

and bridge reporting from Higher Education Research and Science Studies.

Starting point

Data set I: DZHW Science Survey of 2019
Data set II: EUROSTUDENT VII of 2019-2021

Challenges

  • Finding appropriate visualizations to be included in the Barometer with special regard to understandability for laymen and accessibility for people with disabilities but also as an exploratory source for further data analysis.
  • Comparing visualization solutions, aiming at developing recommendations for software choice and drawing conclusions on dataset requirements.
  • Transferring insights gained from visualizing the Science Survey data to the EUROSTUDENT dataset.

Empirical State of Graphics

  • EUROSTUDENT and DZHW Science Survey make report their results as pdf - we have evaluated a poster series and a report which contain 35 and 38 visualizations
  • Bar charts are famous for representations of categorical variables which we often find in social surveys
  • We then chose to aim at improving readability of bar charts with panelized layouts and the colorblind-friendly DZHW color scheme

Recommendations for advanced reporting in the future

Blend text production and data analyses in one workflow

We provide therefore:

  • Report template for .html and .docx reporting
  • R/Shiny-App (Proof of Concept)
  • Guideline and documentation for both

Blended reporting has also further potential (e.g.this Presentation, .pdf/LaTeX reporting, .html/CSS reporting)

The template for reports

  • Clear visualizations with familiar plot types are accessible for laymen.
  • Panelization and stacking allow for visualization of four variables in one bar chart.
  • HTML reports are highly accessible for visually impaired audiences.
  • Alternative texts can easily be added to plots.

However, sometimes we need printable reports with fixed layout.

  • RMarkdown works with the DZHW report template, but manual editing of the Word document is required afterwards.

The guideline with EUROSTUDENT and DZHW Science Survey data

Practical display of RMarkdown applied to EUROSTUDENT VII and DZHW Science Survey data sets. E.g. how to write functions for plots:

stacked_bars_cross <- function(data, column1,column2, ...) {...}

and also some variety on plot functions.

The R/Shiny dashboard

  • Dashboards make use of aggregated data sets which can be made openly available
  • we have prepared a proof of concept
